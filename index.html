Camel

Intresting Facts About Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Chameleon

Intresting Facts About Chameleon

  • Their feet work like salad tongs.
  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar.
  • Chameleons vary wildly in terms of body size.
  • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temprature.

Pigeon

Intresting Facts About Pigeon

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages.
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet, differentiate between photographs, and even distinguish different humans within a photograph.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals.

Teddy-Bear

Intresting Facts About Teddy-Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
  • September 9th is National Teddy Bear Day!
  • Jackie Miley, from Hilly City, South Dakota has collected the world’s biggest teddy bear collection, totaling 8,026 bear friends in 2012.

Panda

Intresting Facts About Panda

  • Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!
  • An adult panda weight more than 45 kilos (100 pounds), and can be about 1.5 meters (5 feet) long!
  • Giant pandas can't stand and do kung fu moves like Po in Kungfu Panda. But they are good tree climbers. They can climb trees from 7 months old.
  • Pandas are born looking like baby badgers — fur-less, pink, and blind. The iconic black and white colour comes later, after about three weeks.

Cicada

Intresting Facts About Cicada

  • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar,it consists of empty, air-filled spaces that can generate sound.
  • Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
  • Cicadas live on all continents except for Antarctica.
  • Cicadas are known to have one of the longest insect life spans.
